# Hook Library
> 11 Categories, 50+ Templates, Ranked by Performance

## Tier 1: Highest Conversion

### A. Identity Call Hooks
Pattern: "If you [are/do/own X], this is for you"
- "If you own a local business and you don't have a website, watch this"
- "If you're a business owner still using Wix, I need 30 seconds"
- "If your website looks like it was built in 2015, keep watching"
- "If you're spending money on ads but your website doesn't convert, this is for you"
- "Stop scrolling if you own a local business"
Why: Instant self-selection. Zero wasted attention.

### B. Contrarian Strike Hooks
Pattern: "Stop doing [common thing]" or "X is killing your business"
- "Stop paying $5,000 for a website"
- "Your business doesn't need more followers. It needs a website that converts"
- "SEO is dead for local businesses. Here's what works now."
- "Stop posting on social media if your website looks like this"
- "Web designers don't want you to know this"
Why: Pattern interrupt. Creates cognitive dissonance they need to resolve.

### C. Open Loop Hooks
Pattern: Introduce an incomplete piece of information
- "This $400 website is outperforming a $10,000 one. Here's why."
- "I just built a website that got a business 47 calls in its first week"
- "One change to this business's website doubled their phone calls"
- "This restaurant was invisible on Google. 30 days later..."
- "I showed this business owner what their competitors' websites look like. His reaction..."
Why: Brain MUST close the loop. 71% decide in first 2-3 seconds.

### D. Confession / Vulnerability Hooks
Pattern: "I [failed/lost/was wrong about] X before learning Y"
- "I lost my first 3 web design clients before I figured this out"
- "I used to charge $200 for websites. Here's why I stopped."
- "I built 50 websites before I learned what actually gets calls"
- "The worst website I ever built taught me the most important lesson"
Why: Vulnerability creates trust. Anti-guru angle.

## Tier 2: Strong Performers

### E. Stat / Number Hooks
Pattern: Lead with a specific, surprising number
- "97% of people check your website before calling. Here's what yours says about you."
- "The average local business loses 63% of potential customers because of their website"
- "I've built 200+ websites. Only these 3 things actually matter."
- "This plumber went from 2 calls a week to 15. One change."
- "76% of local searches lead to a visit within 24 hours. Are you showing up?"
Why: Numbers = credibility. Odd numbers outperform round ones.

### F. Question Hooks
Pattern: Ask something the viewer can't ignore
- "When's the last time you Googled your own business?"
- "What does your website look like on a phone right now?"
- "Would you hire YOUR business based on YOUR website?"
- "How many calls did your website get you this month?"
- "Why do your competitors show up on Google and you don't?"
Why: Forces internal answer. Creates micro-commitment.

### G. "I Just..." / Recency Hooks
Pattern: Imply freshness
- "I just finished this website for a local barber and I'm obsessed"
- "I just got this text from a client. Look at these results."
- "I just redesigned this restaurant's website. Here's the before and after."
- "A business owner just DM'd me this. I had to share."
Why: Recency = relevance. Feels like breaking news.

### H. Before/After Reveal Hooks
Pattern: Show the transformation visually
- "This is what their website looked like before. Now watch this."
- "From THIS... to THIS. Same business. Same budget."
- "Their old website vs. what I built them. The difference?"
- "Website makeover: local plumber edition"
Why: Highest completion rates of any format.

## Tier 3: Situational / Trending

### I. POV Hooks
- "POV: You finally stopped losing customers to your competitor's website"
- "POV: A business owner sees their new website for the first time"
- "POV: You Google your business and you're actually on the first page"
Why: Native social media language. Works well with face-on-camera.

### J. "Nobody talks about this" / Secret Hooks
- "Nobody talks about why most local business websites fail"
- "The real reason your website isn't getting calls (it's not what you think)"
- "What Wix and Squarespace don't tell you about local SEO"
Why: Insider/outsider dynamic. Viewer feels privileged access.

### K. Controversy / Bold Claim Hooks
- "Wix websites are killing local businesses"
- "You don't need a $5,000 website. You need one that works."
- "GoDaddy should be illegal for what they charge small businesses"
- "Your website designer is lying to you"
Why: Triggers emotional response. Comments (agree or disagree) boost algorithm.
