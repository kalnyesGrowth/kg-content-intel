## The Hook (First 5 Seconds)
> "I've seen a lot of people talking about how local businesses are a gold mine for AI and automation services. I really don't think that they are. I scaled my own AI automation agency to 72K a month."

## Why It Works
- **Psychology:** Authority-backed contrarian strike. He doesn't just disagree with the "sell AI to local businesses" narrative, he immediately backs it with $72K/month credentials. This is the "I'm not jealous, I'm successful AND I disagree" position. It short-circuits the viewer's objection ("you're just saying that because you can't do it"). The "Fool's Gold" animated graphic that appears at 4s makes the metaphor visual: what looks like gold (local businesses) is actually worthless. The "despite what gurus claim" subtitle adds the anti-guru framing that resonates with experienced builders.
- **Pattern:** Contrarian Strike + Authority Credential. Lead with the popular opinion, reject it, then prove you have the standing to reject it. The credential comes within the same breath as the contrarian claim.
- **Audience filter:** People already in the AI/automation space who have heard the "sell to local businesses" pitch and are either struggling with it or skeptical. This repels beginners who haven't started yet and attracts operators who've hit the ceiling on local business clients.
