## The Hook (First 5 Seconds)
> "Most local businesses are losing thousands of dollars a month just because they don't have a real website. And the wild part is you can fix that with AI in under 10 minutes, even if you've never coded a thing in your life."

## Why It Works
- **Psychology:** Triple trigger stack. (1) Loss aversion fires first. "Losing thousands of dollars" hits harder than "could be making thousands." The red "-$1,000" text overlay with physical cash in hand makes the loss tangible, not abstract. (2) Identity call. "Even if you've never coded" invites non-technical people in. This is a permission hook. It says "yes, YOU can do this." (3) The "wild part" transition creates a micro open loop right before the promise lands.
- **Pattern:** Pain-first hook with stat quantifier. Lead with the problem (losing money), then pivot to an impossibly easy solution (AI, 10 minutes, no coding). The gap between "thousands lost" and "10 minutes to fix" is so large it forces a "how?" reaction.
- **Audience filter:** Non-technical people looking for a side hustle or new income stream. NOT targeting experienced web designers or agency owners. The "never coded" qualifier explicitly welcomes beginners and repels advanced viewers who would dismiss it.
