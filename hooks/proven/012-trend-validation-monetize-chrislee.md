## The Hook (First 5 Seconds)
> "Everyone says you can build $10,000 websites with AI."

## Why It Works
- **Psychology:** Two-phase curiosity trap. (1) The opening validates a trend the viewer already knows about ("everyone says you can build websites with AI"), which creates immediate recognition and buy-in. Then the visual montage of premium website examples (Apple Watch product page, mechanical keyboard showcase) PROVES the quality is real, removing skepticism. (2) The kinetic text cards ("Single Client" with red X, then "1 Website = $10,000") reveal HIS contrarian monetization angle. He's not teaching HOW to build. He's showing that ONE client, ONE website = $10K. The red X on "Single Client" creates a pattern interrupt that forces the viewer to stay for the reframe.
- **Pattern:** Trend Validation + Contrarian Monetization. Unique among the library because he agrees with the premise (AI websites work) but reframes the business model (not volume, premium pricing per project).
- **Audience filter:** People already building AI websites who want to charge more. The $10K price point attracts ambitious builders, not beginners wondering if AI websites are real.
