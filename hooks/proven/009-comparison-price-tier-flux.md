# Comparison Price Tier (Three-Website Reveal)

## Source
- **Creator:** Flux Academy (YouTube)
- **Video:** "$500 vs $5,000 vs $50,000 Websites"
- **URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GFTDR8_q63M
- **Views:** 416,000
- **Category:** Before-After + Comparison

## The Hook (First 5 Seconds)
> "These three websites were built for the exact same purpose, to help a business grow online. But one of them costs $500, one cost $5,000, and the last one cost $50,000."

## Why It Works
- **Psychology:** Triple curiosity gap. The viewer's brain immediately asks three questions: (1) What does a $50K website look like? (2) Can I tell the difference? (3) Which tier am I actually paying for? The 100x price spread ($500 to $50K) forces the viewer to stay to understand WHY. This also triggers self-assessment: every web designer watching instantly categorizes themselves into one of these tiers. Business owners wonder if they're getting ripped off. Designers wonder if they're undercharging.
- **Pattern:** Comparison + Classification hook. Three tiers create a natural narrative arc (cheap, mid, premium) that gives the video built-in structure. Similar to "beginner vs intermediate vs pro" formats but with dollar amounts adding real stakes.
- **Audience filter:** Anyone who builds, buys, or sells websites. Both supply side (designers comparing their pricing) and demand side (business owners evaluating what they paid). Everyone finds their lane.
