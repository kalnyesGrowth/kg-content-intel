## The Hook (First 5 Seconds)
> "These three websites were built for the exact same purpose, to help a business grow online. But one of them costs $500, one cost $5,000, and the last one cost $50,000."

## Why It Works
- **Psychology:** Triple curiosity gap. The viewer's brain immediately asks three questions: (1) What does a $50K website look like? (2) Can I tell the difference? (3) Which tier am I actually paying for? The price spread is so extreme ($500 to $50K = 100x) that it forces the viewer to stay to understand WHY the difference exists. This also triggers loss aversion, because if you can't tell the difference, you might be overpaying.
- **Pattern:** Comparison hook from hook-formulas.md. Three tiers create a natural narrative arc (cheap, mid, premium) that gives the video built-in structure.
- **Audience filter:** Anyone who builds, buys, or sells websites. Business owners wonder if they're getting ripped off. Designers wonder if they're undercharging. Both self-select in.
